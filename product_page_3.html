Slims

This packaging design is influenced by Kim Kardashian's skims packaging. This design targets consumer who are less informed with recycling symbol meaning. While some people may view this product and believe the statement that there is no plastic found in the packaging, this conclusion is indeed incorrect as the packaging is made from LDPE which is low density polyethylene. This is confirmed by the small LDPE symbol on the bottom of the packaging for legal purposes. LDPE 4 is indeed plastic, it takes the same amount of time to decompose as other plastics.

Giving credit to the design, the material choice of LDPE 4 can be seen as a more “eco-friendly” version of plastic as unlike pla or craft paper, it does not have toxins thus better when recycled [“The Difference”]. However claiming that it is not plastic is simply false. This uses sustainability as a marketing tool to seem more concerned to attract more buyers due to the increasing interest in sustainability (Amelia).

This section further discusses the implications of using LDPE and how it impacts consumer perception. Despite the potential benefits of using a less toxic form of plastic, the misleading claims can damage consumer trust. It's crucial for companies to be transparent about their materials and the actual environmental impact.
