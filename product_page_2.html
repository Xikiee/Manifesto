Detox

This design is inspired by a mixture of design choices made by water brands such as Evian and Poland Springs and also words used in grocery bags by Carrefour in Qatar. Evian and Poland Springs are the top 3 and 4 highest selling water brands in the US as of 2023[Maleha Afzal]. Both of these water brands use the same technique to advertise their product to the consumer.

As seen by the example product image, the design on the packaging alludes feelings associated with nature. These green imagery allow the brand to gain a competitive edge, increase sales, and enhance their reputation [Shareenath].

Previously Poland Springs has received numerous counts of backlash, stating the design of the packaging is manipulative. [Sustainable footprint] argues 'the eco-friendly label eases people's guilt' when purchasing the plastic bottle which is detrimental to the environment. Due to the natural connotations consumers are more likely to look over the harmful environmental impacts in their frequent purchase. This can however be argued, as perhaps, the design is meant to represent where the water comes from rather than trying to seem more green.

The words written however shows more the attempt to appear sustainable. Vague words such as “eco-friendly” and “oxo-biodegradable” do not have clear definitions or standards, therefor could be made without facing legal complications while still benefiting from the possible advantages already mentioned above. These benefits although are not as good as they might initially seem or are no longer true. For example with the case of oxo-biodegradable the word is actually now banned in many European countries due to the “lack of evidence of biodegradability in real-world conditions” [“Products”]. Similarly, the US Federal Trade Commission (FTC) recently updated their green guide for the first time in 10 years which allows the FTC to “take enforcement action against the businesses violating them (the guide).” This includes using vague sustainable sounding words for marketing. The EU is also working to create a similar guide [“Products”]. This depicts how as society is increasingly becoming more aware of the environment and being more proactive, surface level sustainable appeal may actually cause economic hurt for the company due to fines and the need redesign to fit the different guidelines of different countries. On the consumer side, more people are becoming aware of these tactic and it causes trust to be lost for these companies and products.
