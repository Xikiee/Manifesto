Plastic Blend

This product is based on the ambiguous claims that mentos makes with their newly improved more environmentally friendly packaging. Mentos is seen implying this new packaging is more environmentally friendly due to it being composed of 90% 'paper board'. However they fail to mention what the other 10% of the composition is. While paper is commonly known as an easily recyclable and is often associated with being a more sustainable material, this paper board is further mixed with plastic and aluminum. This is understandable as paper itself would not make a durable material for packaging due to its limitations against water and liquids. It is great to see that the designer has made efforts to decrease the amount of plastic material being used.

Despite this however, the design heavily implies and claims that this packaging is recyclable. Experts claim otherwise with statements like “such a blended mix of materials is, in practice, impossible to recycle.” [Changingmarkets]. This creates confusion for the consumer with reference to how it should be disposed. This can also cause issues in the future due to fines as countries are increasing their guidelines on sustainability claims for marketing [”Products”]. This is expanded upon in the effects of greenwashing on the home page. Moreover a possible solution is suggested in the solution product.
